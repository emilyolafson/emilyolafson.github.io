I am a PhD candidate at the Weill Cornell Graduate School of Medical Sciences in the department of Neuroscience working in the Computational Connectomics lab (PI: Amy Kuceyeski). She primarily works on applying novel analysis techniques to understand functional imaging correlates of motor recovery after stroke, and developing machine learning models to predict motor outcomes from acute clinical imaging data. She completed her BSc in Neuroscience at McGill University and is an instructor in the Cornell Prison Education Program where she teaches Neurochemistry of Human Behavior in NY state prisons. She is originally from Toronto, Canada.

You can reach me at emo4002@med.cornell.edu.
